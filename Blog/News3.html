The Importance of Background Checks | Hiring Information Security Candidates

What you see isn’t what you get. At least, not all the time. There are instances when you think you’ve just made a good hire only to find out later than the employee that you’ve just hired isn’t someone you can trust with your company’s resources, reputation and physical security.

Not every employee tells everything or the truth in their resume, which is why a pre-employment background check is vital for the hiring process. It is essentially one of the most critical tasks that you can perform when it comes to protecting your organization’s interest, as the process reveals significant information regarding the history and records of potential candidates who may be taking on substantial responsibility at your company.

Below are some of the reasons why background checks are so important:

1. Protecting Your IT

IT is the heart of every organization. Even the smallest problems can lead to bigger problems. Just imagine this. Your cybersecurity expert is supposed to be responsible for your IT infrastructure. Do you really want someone whose past leaves a lot to be desired to be in charge of your company’s overall information security?

2. Choosing the Right Candidate

Screening potential candidates will help you make a more informed decision. For example, you have two employees with similar credentials. You run a background check on both of them and discover that one of them has a criminal record. This piece of valuable information could stand between hiring the right and wrong candidate. However, if a background check wasn’t performed, you could have hired the wrong person and create risks and vulnerabilities within your organization.

3. Avoid the Cost of Hiring the Wrong Person

Besides being costly, the price of a bad hire usually adds up in a variety of different ways. Reduced morale among employees, additional supervision for said employee, loss of productivity and even damaging your customer relations because of bad impression.

If you hire your new guy and he quits after the first week, you would waste of lot of resources. Doing a background check will prevent this from happening.

4. Protect Your Company Reputation

Ultimately, your company’s reputation takes precedence. Running a background check assures you that the person that your new employee can be trusted with your company’s reputation, even if you’ve never met them before.
